Executive Summary

Comprehensive analysis of 1,152 products from northlineexpress.com cross-referenced against Amazon reveals NLE is primarily a third-party reseller with minimal proprietary brand value.

Of their Amazon catalog, only 34 products (7%) are potentially transferable NLE-owned brands. The remaining 93% consists of reseller positions competing against 51 independent sellers and Amazon 1P.

This demonstrates that brand ownership alone does not guarantee marketplace control.

Key Risks for Acquisition

  • Only 34 products (7%) are potentially transferable NLE brands
  • Only Woodeze has Brand Registry - other "owned" brands have no brand stores
  • Even on Woodeze, NLE only wins Buy Box on 4/12 products (33%)
  • 51 independent competitors already selling the same products
  • Amazon 1P sells 62 products directly - cannot compete with these
  • 85%+ of catalog has no defensible position

Bottom Line

The Amazon business (~$2M claimed revenue) has minimal transferable value. NLE is primarily a reseller competing against 51 other sellers and Amazon itself. Only 34 products across 3 brands may transfer with the business.

Recommendations

  1. Value Amazon business at near-zero - No proprietary products, no brand protection on most SKUs
  2. Focus due diligence on dot-com business - Direct customer relationships, email list, SEO value
  3. Request Seller Central data - Full ASIN list, sales by SKU, Buy Box win rates, profit margins
  4. Verify IP ownership - Confirm trademark ownership for Woodeze, Copperfield, Heat Fab
  5. Consider excluding Amazon from deal - Or price it at FBA inventory cost basis only
